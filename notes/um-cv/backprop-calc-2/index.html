Recall of essential rules

Let XXX be a matrix of size m×nm \times nm×n, and LLL be a scalar function of XXX.

In higher order notation, we introduce the concept of differentials. If LLL is differentiable, then for a small change dXdXdX in the matrix XXX, we have

dL=∑i=1m∑j=1n∂L∂Xi,j dXi,jdL = \sum_{i=1}^m \sum_{j=1}^n \frac{\partial L}{\partial X_{i,j}} \, dX_{i,j} dL=i=1∑m​j=1∑n​∂Xi,j​∂L​dXi,j​

where dXi,jdX_{i,j}dXi,j​ is a small change (well actually this is a linear functional) in the element Xi,jX_{i,j}Xi,j​.

And we define the matrix of partial derivatives as

∂L∂X=[∂L∂X1,1⋯∂L∂X1,n⋮⋱⋮∂L∂Xm,1⋯∂L∂Xm,n]\frac{\partial L}{\partial X} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial L}{\partial X_{1,1}} & \cdots & \frac{\partial L}{\partial X_{1,n}} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \frac{\partial L}{\partial X_{m,1}} & \cdots & \frac{\partial L}{\partial X_{m,n}} \end{bmatrix} ∂X∂L​=​∂X1,1​∂L​⋮∂Xm,1​∂L​​⋯⋱⋯​∂X1,n​∂L​⋮∂Xm,n​∂L​​​

Then we can write the differential as a Frobenius product:

Important Frobenius product

We will use this following formula many times in the next sections.

We can write dLdLdL as a Frobenius product of the gradient of LLL and the differential of XXX.

dL=tr((∂L∂X)⊤ dX)dL = \mathrm{tr}\left(\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial X}\right)^\top \, dX\right) dL=tr((∂X∂L​)⊤dX)

This is obtained by the fact that tr(A⊤B)=Ai,jBi,j\mathrm{tr}(A^\top B)=A_{i,j}B_{i,j}tr(A⊤B)=Ai,j​Bi,j​.

where dXdXdX is a matrix of the same shape as XXX, representing the differential (or a small variation) of XXX.

Trace rules

Tricks
  1. Scalar rule: if aaa is a scalar, then tr(a)=a\mathrm{tr}(a) = atr(a)=a.
  2. Transpose rule: tr(A)=tr(A⊤)\mathrm{tr}(A) = \mathrm{tr}(A^\top)tr(A)=tr(A⊤).
  3. Linearity: tr(A+B)=tr(A)+tr(B)\mathrm{tr}(A + B) = \mathrm{tr}(A) + \mathrm{tr}(B)tr(A+B)=tr(A)+tr(B).
  4. Cyclic rule: tr(AB)=tr(BA)\mathrm{tr}(AB) = \mathrm{tr}(BA)tr(AB)=tr(BA).
  5. Product rule: tr(A⊤(B⊙C))=tr((A⊙B)⊤C)\mathrm{tr}(A^\top (B \odot C)) = \mathrm{tr}((A \odot B)^\top C)tr(A⊤(B⊙C))=tr((A⊙B)⊤C).

Batch normalization differentiation by hand

The gradient of batch normalization is notoriously difficult to calculate. Our method simplify the calculation of the gradient of batch normalization, and we will just apply the rules.

We will define the following variables:

  • XXX: input data, X∈RN×DX \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times D}X∈RN×D
  • μ\muμ : mean of XXX, μ∈RD\mu \in \mathbb{R}^{D}μ∈RD, a row vector
  • σ2\sigma^2σ2 : variance of XXX, σ2∈RD\sigma^2 \in \mathbb{R}^{D}σ2∈RD, a row vector
  • X^\hat{X}X^ : normalized data, X^∈RN×D\hat{X} \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times D}X^∈RN×D
  • 1N\mathbf{1}_N1N​: a column vector of ones, 1N∈RN\mathbf{1}_N \in \mathbb{R}^{N}1N​∈RN
  • LLL: loss function, L∈RL \in \mathbb{R}L∈R

As well as the following parameters:

  • γ\gammaγ : scale parameter, γ∈RD\gamma \in \mathbb{R}^{D}γ∈RD, a row vector
  • β\betaβ : shift parameter, β∈RD\beta \in \mathbb{R}^{D}β∈RD, a row vector

Forward pass

The forward pass of batch normalization is as follows:

μ=1NXiσ2=1N∑i=1N(Xi−μ)2X^=X−μσ2+ϵ\begin{aligned} \mu &= \frac{1}{N} X_i \\ \sigma^2 &= \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N} (X_i - \mu)^2 \\ \hat{X} &= \frac{X - \mu}{\sqrt{\sigma^2 + \epsilon}} \end{aligned} μσ2X^​=N1​Xi​=N1​i=1∑N​(Xi​−μ)2=σ2+ϵ​X−μ​​

Vectorized form:

μ=1N1N⊤Xσ2=1N1N⊤(X−1Nμ)2X^=X−1Nμ1Nσ2+ϵY=(1Nγ)⊙X^+1Nβ\begin{aligned} \mu &= \frac{1}{N} \mathbf{1}_N^\top X \\ \sigma^2 &= \frac{1}{N} \mathbf{1}_N^\top (X - \mathbf{1}_N\mu)^2 \\ \hat{X} &= \frac{X - \mathbf{1}_N\mu}{\sqrt{\mathbf{1}_N\sigma^2 + \epsilon}} \\ Y &= (\mathbf{1}_N \gamma) \odot\hat{X} + \mathbf{1}_N\beta \end{aligned} μσ2X^Y​=N1​1N⊤​X=N1​1N⊤​(X−1N​μ)2=1N​σ2+ϵ​X−1N​μ​=(1N​γ)⊙X^+1N​β​

Backward pass

Suppose we have the upstream gradient ∂L∂Y\frac{\partial L}{\partial Y}∂Y∂L​

Step 1: Backpropagate Y

Calculate ∂L∂β\frac{\partial L}{\partial \beta}∂β∂L​, ∂L∂γ\frac{\partial L}{\partial \gamma}∂γ∂L​, ∂L∂X^\frac{\partial L}{\partial \hat{X}}∂X^∂L​

Recall the formula of YYY:

Y=(1Nγ)⊙X^+1NβY = (\mathbf{1}_N \gamma) \odot\hat{X} + \mathbf{1}_N\beta Y=(1N​γ)⊙X^+1N​β

We have the differential of YYY:

dY=(1Ndγ)⊙X^+(1Nγ)⊙dX^+1NdβdY = (\mathbf{1}_N d\gamma) \odot\hat{X} + (\mathbf{1}_N \gamma) \odot d\hat{X} + \mathbf{1}_N d\beta dY=(1N​dγ)⊙X^+(1N​γ)⊙dX^+1N​dβ

We can write the differential of LLL as a Frobenius product:

dL=tr((∂L∂Y)⊤dY)=tr((∂L∂Y)⊤((1Ndγ)⊙X^+(1Nγ)⊙dX^+1Ndβ))=tr((∂L∂Y)⊤(1Ndγ)⊙X^)+tr((∂L∂Y)⊤(1Nγ)⊙dX^)+tr((∂L∂Y)⊤1Ndβ)=tr((1N⊤∂L∂Y)⊤dγ⊙X^)+tr((∂L∂Y)⊤(1Nγ)⊙dX^)+tr((∂L∂Y)⊤1Ndβ)\begin{aligned} dL &= \mathrm{tr}\left(\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial Y}\right)^\top dY\right) \\ &= \mathrm{tr}\left(\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial Y}\right)^\top\left((\mathbf{1}_N d\gamma) \odot\hat{X} + (\mathbf{1}_N \gamma) \odot d\hat{X} + \mathbf{1}_N d\beta\right)\right) \\ &= \mathrm{tr}\left(\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial Y}\right)^\top (\mathbf{1}_N d\gamma) \odot\hat{X}\right) + \mathrm{tr}\left(\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial Y}\right)^\top (\mathbf{1}_N \gamma) \odot d\hat{X}\right) + \mathrm{tr}\left(\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial Y}\right)^\top \mathbf{1}_N d\beta\right) \\ &= \mathrm{tr}\left(\left(\mathbf{1}_N^\top\frac{\partial L}{\partial Y}\right)^\top d\gamma \odot\hat{X}\right) + \mathrm{tr}\left(\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial Y}\right)^\top (\mathbf{1}_N \gamma) \odot d\hat{X}\right) + \mathrm{tr}\left(\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial Y}\right)^\top \mathbf{1}_N d\beta\right) \\ \end{aligned} dL​=tr((∂Y∂L​)⊤dY)=tr((∂Y∂L​)⊤((1N​dγ)⊙X^+(1N​γ)⊙dX^+1N​dβ))=tr((∂Y∂L​)⊤(1N​dγ)⊙X^)+tr((∂Y∂L​)⊤(1N​γ)⊙dX^)+tr((∂Y∂L​)⊤1N​dβ)=tr((1N⊤​∂Y∂L​)⊤dγ⊙X^)+tr((∂Y∂L​)⊤(1N​γ)⊙dX^)+tr((∂Y∂L​)⊤1N​dβ)​

For the first term, we have:

dL′=tr((1N⊤∂L∂Y)⊤dγ⊙X^)=tr((1N⊤∂L∂Y)⊤X^⊙dγ)=tr((X^⊤1N⊤∂L∂Y)⊤dγ)\begin{aligned} dL' &= \mathrm{tr}\left(\left(\mathbf{1}_N^\top\frac{\partial L}{\partial Y}\right)^\top d\gamma \odot\hat{X}\right) \\ &= \mathrm{tr}\left(\left(\mathbf{1}_N^\top\frac{\partial L}{\partial Y}\right)^\top \hat{X} \odot d\gamma \right) \\ &= \mathrm{tr}\left(\left(\hat{X}^\top\mathbf{1}_N^\top\frac{\partial L}{\partial Y}\right)^\top d\gamma \right) \\ \end{aligned} dL′​=tr((1N⊤​∂Y∂L​)⊤dγ⊙X^)=tr((1N⊤​∂Y∂L​)⊤X^⊙dγ)=tr((X^⊤1N⊤​∂Y∂L​)⊤dγ)​

Thus, ∂L∂γ=X^⊤1N⊤∂L∂Y\frac{\partial L}{\partial \gamma} = \hat{X}^\top\mathbf{1}_N^\top\frac{\partial L}{\partial Y}∂γ∂L​=X^⊤1N⊤​∂Y∂L​

For the second term, we have:

dL′′=tr((∂L∂Y)⊤(1Nγ)⊙dX^)\begin{aligned} dL'' &= \mathrm{tr}\left(\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial Y}\right)^\top (\mathbf{1}_N \gamma) \odot d\hat{X}\right) \\ \end{aligned} dL′′​=tr((∂Y∂L​)⊤(1N​γ)⊙dX^)​

by applying the product rule, we have:

dL′′=tr((∂L∂Y)⊤(1Nγ)⊙dX^)=tr((∂L∂Y⊙1Nγ)⊤dX^)\begin{aligned} dL'' &= \mathrm{tr}\left(\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial Y}\right)^\top (\mathbf{1}_N \gamma) \odot d\hat{X}\right) \\ &= \mathrm{tr}\left(\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial Y} \odot \mathbf{1}_N \gamma\right)^\top d\hat{X}\right) \\ \end{aligned} dL′′​=tr((∂Y∂L​)⊤(1N​γ)⊙dX^)=tr((∂Y∂L​⊙1N​γ)⊤dX^)​

Thus ∂L∂X^=∂L∂Y⊙1Nγ\frac{\partial L}{\partial \hat{X}} = \frac{\partial L}{\partial Y} \odot \mathbf{1}_N \gamma∂X^∂L​=∂Y∂L​⊙1N​γ

For the third term, similarly, we have:

∂L∂β=1N⊤∂L∂Y\begin{aligned} \frac{\partial L}{\partial \beta} &= \mathbf{1}_N^\top\frac{\partial L}{\partial Y} \end{aligned} ∂β∂L​​=1N⊤​∂Y∂L​​

Now that we have ∂L∂X^\frac{\partial L}{\partial \hat{X}}∂X^∂L​, we can calculate ∂L∂μ\frac{\partial L}{\partial \mu}∂μ∂L​ and ∂L∂σ2\frac{\partial L}{\partial \sigma^2}∂σ2∂L​.

Step 3: Backpropagate σ2

Recall the formula of σ2\sigma^2σ2:

σ2=1N1N⊤(X−1Nμ)2\begin{aligned} \sigma^2 &= \frac{1}{N} \mathbf{1}_N^\top (X - \mathbf{1}_N\mu)^2 \\ \end{aligned} σ2​=N1​1N⊤​(X−1N​μ)2​

Hence,
